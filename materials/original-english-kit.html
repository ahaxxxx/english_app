Vocabulary

词块与复现训练

先学能反复用到的表达，而不是零散背单词。下面两组词适合科研、学术阅读、口语表达和日常复盘。

Academic Action Verbs

  • analyze: break a problem into smaller parts
  • compare: examine two or more items side by side
  • estimate: give a careful rough number
  • justify: explain why a choice is reasonable
  • revise: improve after feedback

Speaking Control Phrases

  • To put it simply: simplify an idea
  • What I mean is: repair unclear speech
  • A better example would be: replace a weak example
  • The key point is that: refocus the answer
  • From my perspective: move into opinion mode

Reading

原创精读材料

Passage A: Why Short Daily Sessions Work Better

Many learners believe that long study sessions show stronger commitment. In reality, consistency often matters more than intensity. A ninety-minute session can feel productive, but it is also easy to postpone. A smaller session, such as twenty minutes of focused listening or ten minutes of speaking practice, creates less resistance. The learner starts before doubt has time to grow.

Short sessions are also easier to repeat. Repetition builds familiarity, and familiarity lowers mental friction. When a task becomes routine, the brain spends less energy on decision-making and more energy on the task itself. This is why daily practice can quietly outperform dramatic effort. It may look less impressive from the outside, but it produces more contact hours over time.

Questions: 1. Why can a shorter session reduce resistance? 2. What does “mental friction” mean in this passage? 3. Summarize the main idea in 30 words.
Passage B: Feedback Is Useful Only When It Changes Behavior

Many students collect corrections but do not change their habits. They underline mistakes, save screenshots, and promise to review them later. However, feedback has little value if it never enters the next round of practice. Real improvement begins when a learner converts feedback into a narrow, visible action. For example, instead of writing “grammar problem” in a notebook, the learner can write “check verb tense in the second sentence before submitting.”

This kind of feedback is specific, memorable, and measurable. It reduces confusion and gives the learner one clear target. Over time, small corrections become stable behavior. The final goal is not to collect more comments. The goal is to make fewer repeat errors.

Questions: 1. What is the difference between collecting feedback and using feedback? 2. Why is a narrow action more effective? 3. Write one sentence about a repeat error you want to reduce.
Speaking

跟读与口语输出脚本

Script 1: Daily Reset

When I miss a study day, I try not to turn it into a dramatic failure. I look at the next available hour and restart with a smaller task. My goal is not to feel perfect. My goal is to return quickly and keep my routine alive.

Script 2: Learning Through Output

Input gives me examples, but output shows me what I truly know. When I speak or write, I discover missing words, weak logic, and unclear grammar. That discomfort is useful because it tells me what to practice next.

Script 3: Focused Reading

I no longer read only for completion. I read to notice structure, transitions, and reusable expressions. Even one short paragraph can teach me a lot if I slow down enough to observe it carefully.

Task How to do it
Shadowing 先听自己读，再对照文本纠正停顿、重音和连读。
Retelling 合上文本，用自己的话复述主要观点，允许换词但不要丢逻辑。
Timed output 设定 60 秒，围绕脚本主题说自己的经历或看法。
今日口语任务： 任选一段脚本，跟读 3 轮，再录 1 段 60 秒扩展回答，主题可以是“最近一次学习中断后如何恢复”。
Writing

原创写作模板与提示

Summary Frame

The passage argues that _____. It explains this point by showing that _____. Overall, the writer suggests that _____.

Opinion Frame

From my perspective, _____. The main reason is that _____. A useful example is _____. For this reason, I believe _____.

Reflection Frame

Today I did well in _____. However, I still need to improve _____. Tomorrow I will focus on one specific action: _____.

Email Frame

Dear ____, I am writing to ask about _____. I would appreciate it if you could clarify _____. Thank you for your time and support.

Daily Writing Prompts:
  • Describe one learning habit that saves time but still feels difficult to maintain.
  • Explain why speaking practice often reveals weaknesses faster than reading.
  • Write a short reflection on one mistake you repeated this week.
